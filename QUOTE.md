### More Classical Chan/Zen Texts

_(all available in good Cleary translations)_

1. **The Book of Serenity (Shōyōroku)**
    
    - Another 100-case kōan collection, a bit gentler and more poetic than _Blue Cliff_.
    - Cleary’s translation is excellent and pairs beautifully with _Blue Cliff_ for contrasting styles.
2. **The Gateless Gate (Mumonkan)**
    
    - 48 iconic kōans with Wumen’s succinct comments and verses.
    - Cleary’s version keeps the sharp Zen humor and paradox.
3. **The Record of Linji (Lin-chi Lu)**
    
    - Fiery, direct teachings from Master Linji (Rinzai).
    - Full of his famous shouts and strikingly practical advice.
4. **The Sayings of Layman P’ang**
    
    - Intimate, everyday Chan expressed in poetry and dialogue by a lay practitioner and his daughter.

---

### Broader Chan/Chinese Buddhist Classics

5. **Transmission of the Lamp (Ching-te Chuan-teng Lu)**
    
    - Early lineage stories of the Chan patriarchs.
    - Cleary translated a concise selection that’s very approachable.
6. **Instant Zen: Wumen Huikai’s Teaching**
    
    - A direct, occasionally playful companion to the _Gateless Gate_ compiler’s other talks.
7. **Original Teachings of Ch’an Buddhism (Selections from the Platform Sutra, Huangbo, etc.)**
    
    - Cleary collected several key masters in one volume.

---

### Japanese Zen & Related Voices

8. **Dōgen’s Treasury of the True Dharma Eye (Shōbōgenzō)** – Cleary has a single-volume selection if you’d like a sampler before diving into the full work.
9. **Zen Letters (Yunmen, Fayan, and others)** – pithy, practice-oriented letters and talks.
